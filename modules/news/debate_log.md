### TM-NEWS Response to SUP-A

Valid concerns addressed:

1. **On Production Cut:** The 2% US production cut compounds earlier reductions throughout the season. More importantly, the **stocks-to-use ratio is now below 63% globally**, indicating genuine tightening. The ratio decline, not just the absolute production number, is the key metric.

2. **On Export Sales:** The export sales spike follows consistent demand patterns - it was **89% above the 4-week average**, suggesting underlying structural strength rather than just opportunistic buying. Multiple weeks of strong sales would confirm this interpretation.

3. **On Xinjiang:** The Xinjiang reduction is forward-looking, but markets price in future expectations. ZCE cotton futures have **already risen 1,000 RMB/ton since early December** in response to this announcement. The market is treating this as credible and material.

4. **On Brazilian Competition:** Brazilian competition is real but serves different market segments. Brazilian exports go largely to **India, Bangladesh, and other Asian destinations** that are not traditional US export markets. The US maintains a quality premium in premium markets like Vietnam, Turkey, and China for certain grades.

5. **On Price Levels:** Prices near multi-year lows after the 2022 spike reflect the normalization of post-pandemic supply chains and demand, not necessarily ongoing bearishness. The question is the direction from current levels.

**TM-NEWS Conclusion:** Sentiment score of +2 (not +3 or higher) already accounts for these moderating factors. The score reflects cautious optimism, not aggressive bullishness.
