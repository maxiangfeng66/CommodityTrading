## Consensus View

The market consensus holds that cotton will remain under pressure in 2026 due to:

1. **Global Oversupply**: Production at 119.4 million bales (8-year high) exceeds consumption
2. **Elevated Inventories**: Ending stocks at 78-92 million bales; stocks-to-use ratio 80-82%
3. **Weak Demand**: Textile consumption soft across China, Vietnam, Bangladesh, Turkey
4. **Competition**: Brazil surpassed US as top exporter; synthetic fibers gaining share

Most analysts expect prices between 58-65 cents/lb unless a major weather event disrupts production.

## Logic Assessment

### Is the Market Logic Sound?

**Verdict: SOUND** (with critical caveats)

The bearish fundamental thesis is logically consistent:
- Supply > Demand (verifiable)
- High stocks = price pressure (historically validated)
- Weak demand = limited upside (observable)

**However**, sound logic does not guarantee correct forecasts. The market has priced maximum bearishness (39 weeks net short, 46.8% short interest). Following sound logic into a crowded trade may produce negative expected value.
