> "Brazil has now overtaken the US as the world's top cotton exporter with a weak currency advantage. This competitive pressure is structural, not temporary."

Partially valid. The sentiment improvement (79% positive/neutral in AmCham survey) represents meaningful directional change even if structural barriers persist. The Phase Two deal framework provides a pathway for further reductions, but I acknowledge the current barriers remain substantial.

**Response to Challenge 4:**
Accepted. Brazil's structural advantage with record production and weak Real is a significant medium-term bearish factor that I underweighted in my initial assessment. This requires score adjustment.
